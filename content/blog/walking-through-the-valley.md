# Walking Through the Valley

> *"Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me."* — **Psalm 23:4**

Every song I have ever written that mattered was born in a valley.

Not the mountaintops — those produce **anthems**, but they rarely produce **truth**. Truth is forged when the lights go out and all you can hear is your own breathing and the still, small voice that refuses to leave the room.

## The shepherd doesn't carry a flashlight

I used to read this Psalm and quietly wish God would *just turn the lights on*. Show me the way out. Make the valley a meadow. But the promise isn't *no valley* — it's *"you are with me"*.

That changes the music entirely.

## A practice for the valley

When the season is heavy, I do three things:

- **Name the valley.** Out loud, in a journal, in a voice memo. God is not afraid of honesty.
- **Sing one true line.** Not a whole song. Just one line you believe.
- **Stay in the room.** Don't run from the silence. Some of the best melodies live there.

The shepherd is already with you. The song is already being written.

Let it be sung.

— *Leo*
