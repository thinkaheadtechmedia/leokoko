# Why I Sing in Swahili

> *"Every nation, tribe, people and language, standing before the throne…"* — **Revelation 7:9**

There is a language your **mind** speaks, and a language your **soul** prays in. For me — they are not the same.

## The first prayer I ever heard

My grandmother prayed in Kiswahili at 4am every morning. I didn't always understand the words, but I understood the **weight**. The way the room got quiet. The way the air felt different when she said *Bwana asifiwe*.

That is what I am trying to put into songs.

## Heaven is multilingual

When John saw the throne room, he didn't see one language. He saw **every** language, all singing together. That tells me my mother tongue isn't a cultural accessory to my faith — it's a thread in the tapestry God is weaving.

So I sing in English. And I sing in Swahili. And sometimes, in the same song, I switch — because that's how my soul actually talks to God.

If a Swahili lyric ever wrecks you and you don't know what it means, *good*. The Holy Spirit was never bound by Strong's Concordance.

— *Leo*
